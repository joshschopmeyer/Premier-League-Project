Getting Started¶

In this project, we aim to evaluate the performance of teams in the English Premier League during the 2018–2019 season. Our goal is to understand how different factors contributed to team success or struggles throughout the season. By analyzing match statistics such as points per game, goals scored at home, and performance differences between home and away matches, we can gain deeper insight into what influenced each club’s standing. Specifically, we investigate patterns such as home-field advantage, scoring efficiency, and consistency across venues to determine which metrics best explain overall team performance. We can also analyze certain statistics and trends in data to determine what statistics are most valuable and contribute most to winning games and scoring goals. Through visualizations and statistical analysis, this project reveals the dynamics that defined one of the world’s most competitive football leagues.

Next, let's take a look at some more in-play specific stats. We already determined that goals were crucial to winning games in the Premier League; however, how are these goals created? They are created through shots on goal, and in order for the shot to go in, it is a requirement for it to at least be on target, meaning its trajectory was headed within the goal. This does not account for whether the shot went in or not, however. We'll be looking at which Premier League teams were the most accurate with their shots, and if it played a significant role in team and scoring success.

The missing factor from before may be the conversion of these shots on target into actual goals. As we can see, Arsenal, Liverpool, and Manchester City, three common successful names in this analysis once again rank at the top of the list, while now our 3 bottom teams from the table have moved back down to the very bottom of the list, suggesting this metric is more useful and indicative than shot accuracy.
